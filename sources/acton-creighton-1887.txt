Lord Acton to Bishop Mandell Creighton, 5 April 1887.

I cannot accept your canon that we are to judge Pope and King unlike
other men, with a favourable presumption that they did no wrong. If
there is any presumption it is the other way against holders of power,
increasing as the power increases. Power tends to corrupt and absolute
power corrupts absolutely. Great men are almost always bad men, even
when they exercise influence and not authority: still more when you
superadd the tendency or the certainty of corruption by authority.
There is no worse heresy than that the office sanctifies the holder of
it.
